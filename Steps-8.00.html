Comparison of ROI:



The chart illustrates two distinct investment strategies aimed at improving wines with quality scores below 5:

  1. Alcohol Content Boost (Case 1): By investing $10,000 into increasing the alcohol content, the expectation is to see a quality rise by 0.444 per score increment, which could generate a potential revenue of $98,800 and deliver a high ROI of 888%. This substantial ROI highlights the strategy's effectiveness in possibly enhancing consumer perception of the wine's quality.
  2. Acidity Reduction (Case 2): The strategy here involves a larger investment of $30,000 to reduce volatile acidity, enhancing quality and market perception. Although the expected quality improvement is more modest at 0.266 per score increment, the strategy may yield potential revenue of $63,200 and an ROI of 110.67%. Despite a lower ROI than the first case, this strategy emphasizes strengthening brand loyalty and customer retention.
Both strategies demonstrate proactive approaches to improving wine quality and market position, with varying degrees of investment and anticipated returns. Case 1 offers a high ROI with substantial potential revenue, aligning with boosting consumer perception, while Case 2 trades a higher investment for increased customer loyalty, despite a lower ROI.
