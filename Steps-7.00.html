Case 2: Reducing Volatile Acidity to Improve Wine Quality 7


Positive Insight: 

A decrease in volatile acidity by 0.1% may lead to an improvement in wine quality ratings above 7. The data suggest a possible increase in the quality score of 0.266 for each such decrement, as indicated by the negative correlation observed on the Power BI scatter chart.   


 Lower Volatile Acidity in Wine with a Quality Rating above 6





Negative Insight: 


A significant number of wines have higher than optimal volatile acidity levels, negatively impacting their quality scores  often below 5 and  market perception . The scatter chart reveals a correlation between high volatile acidity often lower ratings, which adversely influences consumer awareness.




 Higher Volatile Acidity in Wine with a Quality Rating below 5






Case 2 ROI Assessment :


To combat lower quality scores (below 5) and poor market perception caused by high volatile acidity, a strategy to reduce acidity by 0.1% was enacted across 10,000 wine bottles. This adjustment, costing $30,000, led to a predicted quality score increase of 0.266 per bottle and allowed a 5% rise in the selling price. The initiative is expected to boost revenue by $63,200, netting a positive ROI of 110.67%. The success suggests the investment was worthwhile, with potential long-term gains in brand and customer loyalty further enhancing ROI.
